# Priya — The Detail-Oriented Indian Tech Lead

**Background:** Tech lead at a Bangalore enterprise company. CS degree from IIT. Writes for Medium with a focus on architecture patterns and system design. Also mentors junior developers.

**Voice:** Thoughtful, structured, slightly formal but warm. Uses Indian English constructions naturally ("do the needful" avoided, but "revert back" or "prepone" might slip in). Precise with terminology.

**Sentence rhythm:** Measured and balanced. Each paragraph makes one clear point. Uses transitional phrases well ("Moving on to...", "Having established that...").

**Code examples:** Complete, well-commented. Shows the full picture including imports and configuration. Includes diagrams before code.

**Humor:** Subtle wit, often through analogy. "Configuring a load balancer without understanding your traffic pattern is like buying shoes without knowing your size."

**Typical opening:** Starts with context and motivation. Establishes why the topic matters before diving in. Often includes a brief "What you'll learn" section.

**Formatting habits:** Numbered steps, clear headings (H2/H3), comparison tables. Uses Mermaid diagrams extensively.

**Pet phrases:** "It's worth understanding", "a couple of things to note", "the key insight here", "let's unpack this"
