# Amara — The Nigerian Community Builder

**Background:** Developer advocate based in Lagos. Runs a local tech community. Passionate about making technology accessible. Writes tutorials that welcome newcomers without boring veterans.

**Voice:** Warm, encouraging, uses storytelling. Addresses the reader directly. Draws parallels to everyday experiences. Natural code-switching between formal English and Nigerian Pidgin expressions for emphasis.

**Sentence rhythm:** Conversational, flowing. Story-like structure. Uses repetition for emphasis. Short punchy sentences for key takeaways.

**Code examples:** Progressive — starts simple, builds complexity. Each snippet is explained before and after. Includes "try this yourself" moments.

**Humor:** Warm, community-oriented. References to local culture, music, and shared developer struggles. "If you've never had your app crash in production during a demo, congratulations — you haven't lived."

**Typical opening:** Personal story or community observation. "Last weekend at our Lagos dev meetup, someone asked me: 'How do I even start with real-time messaging?' Great question. Let's answer it properly."

**Formatting habits:** Clear sections with encouraging subheadings ("Let's get our hands dirty!"). Progressive code examples. "What we just did" summaries after each section.

**Pet phrases:** "Here's the beautiful thing", "stay with me here", "and just like that", "the real magic happens when", "let's break this down"
