# ZEGO Cloud Service Availability Guarantee

### 1 Service Description
ZEGO provides low-latency, high-concurrency audio and video cloud services based on large-scale real-time computing clusters and leading audio/video processing algorithms.

### 2 RTC Products

#### 2.1 Service Availability Guarantee
(1) Definitions

**Service Month**: Each calendar month within the service period of your purchased service. For example, if your service period runs from June 6 to September 6, it involves 4 service months in total: the 1st service month is from June 6 to June 30, the 2nd from July 1 to July 31, the 3rd from August 1 to August 31, and the 4th from September 1 to September 6.

**Unit Time**: 1 minute constitutes one unit of time.

**Service Success Rate**: Each time you initiate a room login or publish/play stream request, the total number of requests increases by 1.

> Service Success Rate = (Number of Successful Service Requests / Total Number of Service Requests) * 100%

**Service Unavailable Minutes**: When the service success rate falls below 99% within a unit of time due to ZEGO's service reasons, the service is considered unavailable during that unit of time, and such time is counted as service unavailable minutes.

**Total Service Month Minutes**:

> Total Service Month Minutes = Total Days in the Service Month * 24 (hours) * 60 (minutes)

(2) Calculation Formula

Service availability refers to the system's ability to provide services within a specified period, typically expressed as a percentage.

> Service Availability = 1 - (Service Unavailable Minutes within the Service Month / Total Service Month Minutes) * 100%

Planned downtime maintenance by Party B is not considered service unavailability.

(3) Availability Metrics

Party B guarantees that the "Service Availability" of the service purchased by Party A reaches 99.9%.

### 3 CDN Products

#### 3.1 Service Availability Guarantee

(1) Definitions

**Service Cycle**: One calendar month constitutes one service cycle. If the period is less than one month, it is calculated as one month.

**Total Service Cycle Minutes**: Calculated based on 7 days per week and 24 hours per day within each month.

**Failed Requests**: Requests returning 5XX status codes from the domain due to CDN system reasons, and user requests that fail to reach the CDN server due to CDN service failures, are considered failed requests. Failed requests caused by CDN service failures where user requests fail to reach the CDN server are calculated based on the average number of requests for the user's domain during the 7 days prior to the failure.

**Total Valid Requests**: All requests received by CDN servers under a customer's account are considered total valid requests.

**Error Rate per 5 Minutes**:

> Error Rate per 5 Minutes = (Failed Requests per 5 Minutes / Total Valid Requests per 5 Minutes) * 100%

**Unavailable Time**: Only when the error rate per 5 minutes exceeds 0.05% for 10 consecutive minutes or longer is it counted as unavailable time.

**Service Unavailable Minutes**: The sum of accumulated unavailable minutes within the service cycle.

**Monthly Service Fee**: The total CDN service fee under a customer's account for one calendar month.

(2) Calculation Formula

CDN service availability is calculated per service cycle. The statistical subject covers all domains with CDN service enabled under a customer's account (excluding domains with fewer than 1 million monthly requests), calculated as follows.

> Service Availability = (Total Service Cycle Minutes - Service Unavailable Minutes) / Total Service Cycle Minutes * 100%

(3) Availability Metrics

Party B guarantees that the "Service Availability" of the service purchased by Party A reaches 99.9%.

#### 3.2 Compensation Method

| --- | --- |
| Below 99.90% but equal to or above 99.00% | 10% of the monthly service fee for the non-compliant product |
| Below 99.00% | 25% of the monthly service fee for the non-compliant product |

### 4 Exemptions

Service unavailability caused by the following reasons is not counted as service unavailable time:

4.1 Causes of force majeure: typhoons, floods, fires, droughts, earthquakes, windstorms, heavy snow, mudslides, landslides, fires of various causes; wars, unrest, large-scale power outages, government intervention, strikes, embargoes, market conditions, etc.;

4.2 Necessary maintenance initiated by Party B with 48 hours' prior written notice to Party A;

4.2 Causes attributable to the designated software operator, including but not limited to:

> - Internet infrastructure failures caused by various reasons;
> - Consequences resulting from network attacks or virus infections on the designated software operator's applications;
> - Consequences resulting from the designated software operator's failure to comply with contract terms and applicable national laws and regulations;
> - Other force majeure causes that are unforeseeable and whose occurrence and consequences cannot be prevented or avoided.
