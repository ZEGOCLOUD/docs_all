# ZEGO Security and Compliance White Paper


### 1 Introduction

In June 2015, Shenzhen ZEGO Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "ZEGO") was officially established as a global cloud communication service provider. Enterprises and developers can obtain real-time audio and video communication capabilities with a low barrier to entry by integrating ZEGO's services, achieving high-quality audio and video as well as stability and reliability, even in weak network conditions.

Since its inception, the company has focused on developing its own audio and video engine, achieving world-class standards in audio preprocessing, network adaptability, and cross-platform compatibility. At the same time, by fully leveraging the capabilities of fundamental cloud service providers, ZEGO has built the MSDN massive ordered self-learning data network, covering services globally across hundreds of audio and video interactive business scenarios, with daily usage exceeding 3 billion minutes.

To help developers and customers better understand ZEGO's security and privacy protection capabilities, we have compiled the ZEGO Security and Compliance White Paper (hereinafter referred to as the "White Paper") to introduce the security and compliance capabilities of our products and services.

This White Paper applies to enterprise and individual customers (hereinafter collectively referred to as "you") of different types and sizes who use ZEGO services. From this White Paper, you can learn about ZEGO's information security and privacy protection framework as well as specific security measures.

As a global cloud communication service provider, ZEGO is committed to providing customers with a flexible, real-time, scalable, secure, and easy-to-use managed environment.

### 3 Security Organization and Personnel

#### 3.1 Security Organization

ZEGO fully recognizes that in the era of big data, diversified information storage brings new challenges to enterprises. To protect the security of ZEGO itself and the data security of developers and users, ZEGO has established a dedicated information security and privacy protection management organization and positions, with clearly defined responsibilities and authorities, to ensure the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of ZEGO's information security and privacy protection system. Additionally, ZEGO has appointed a Global Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee and be responsible for ZEGO's global data security, privacy protection, and compliance-related work.

#### 3.2 Personnel Management

ZEGO has implemented the following specific security control measures at different stages of the employee lifecycle — before employment, during employment, and upon termination — to establish information security awareness among all employees, clearly communicate internal information control requirements, standardize information security and privacy protection in personnel management, strengthen internal orderly management, and reduce potential risks that personnel may pose to business continuity and security:

1. Pre-employment: ZEGO engages third-party independent professional agencies to verify the authenticity of education, previous work experience, and on-the-job performance of prospective employees based on role characteristics, and conducts background checks. Only after confirming that their background is verified can employment procedures be initiated.

2. During employment: When employees join, in addition to signing an employment contract, ZEGO requires them to sign confidentiality agreements and intellectual property agreements, with confidentiality and IP requirements remaining effective after departure. During employment, employees must comply with ZEGO's security and privacy protection management requirements, must not disclose confidential company information or customer personal information without authorization, and must regularly participate in ZEGO's employee information security and privacy awareness training.

3. Termination: After an employee submits their resignation, the departure must follow ZEGO's defined employee termination process, ensuring timely revocation of logical and physical access permissions and recovery of related IT assets, to mitigate the risks to organizational security caused by personnel changes.

#### 3.3 Security Awareness Training and Communication

ZEGO conducts security awareness training and communication from multiple dimensions, including new employee onboarding training, on-the-job security training, and promotional campaigns, to improve employee information security and privacy protection awareness, reduce the risk and impact of cybersecurity violations, and ensure normal business operations:

1. New employee onboarding training: Upon joining, employees uniformly receive in-person security and privacy protection training, and must complete corresponding online training and assessments during the probationary period.

2. On-the-job security training: ZEGO provides targeted security training at different frequencies for employees responsible for different business modules, focusing on cybersecurity awareness, customer data and privacy protection requirements, etc., and regularly updates course content and examination outlines based on business characteristics.

3. Themed promotional campaigns: ZEGO regularly conducts information security and privacy protection awareness campaigns for all employees, including but not limited to promotion of typical cases and study of laws and regulations related to cybersecurity and privacy protection.

#### 3.4 Security Violation Accountability and Security Rewards

ZEGO has established an internal information security accountability mechanism. For behaviors that violate information security policies and standards, accountability is enforced based on the severity, nature, and consequences of the violation. Those who violate national laws will be held legally responsible.

At the same time, ZEGO has established an internal reward mechanism to encourage employees to proactively report security risks or system vulnerabilities they discover. Rewards are assessed based on the severity, nature, and avoidable or reducible losses of the reported issues, to reduce ZEGO's internal and product security risks and vulnerabilities, improve ZEGO product security, and foster a positive internal security culture.

#### 3.5 Third-Party Security Management

To strengthen the management of suppliers/service providers (hereinafter collectively referred to as "third parties"), ZEGO has established security control measures in the dimensions of third-party assessment and onboarding, due diligence, service security management, service monitoring and evaluation, and service termination, to reduce security risks, prevent the loss of company information assets, and protect customer data and privacy.

1. Third-party assessment and onboarding: Before introducing third-party services, ZEGO conducts risk identification and assessment based on service content, scope, and nature, and develops corresponding risk treatment measures, ensuring that the introduction of third-party activities does not increase the overall residual security risk.

2. Third-party due diligence: For significant third parties, ZEGO conducts in-depth due diligence before signing contracts, including but not limited to: service capabilities and supporting technologies, service experience, personnel skills, market reputation, regulatory ratings, and business continuity status.

3. Service contracts and requirements: When signing service contracts with third parties, agreements include service scope, service content, service levels, security and data protection responsibilities of both parties, and breach and compensation terms.

4. Service security management: ZEGO has established comprehensive information security control measures to prevent risks such as information leakage, information tampering, information unavailability, unauthorized intrusion, and physical environment or facility damage caused by third-party activities, for example: third-party personnel access control, third-party service change management, etc.

5. Service monitoring and evaluation: ZEGO continuously monitors third-party service processes, conducts regular security reviews, and obtains self-assessment reports or reports from independent audit institutions.

6. Service interruption or termination: To mitigate the risk of important third-party service interruption or termination, ZEGO has developed third-party service business continuity management plans and contingency measures to reduce the impact of third-party service emergencies on the organization.


### 4 Development Security

ZEGO organically integrates security and privacy protection requirements into the existing product development lifecycle, implementing corresponding security control measures at various stages of the product lifecycle — including requirements analysis and review, design, coding, testing, and maintenance — as well as in source code management, to reduce the number of vulnerabilities in products, achieve "shift left" security, lower vulnerability remediation costs, minimize risks, build more secure software components, and address security compliance requirements.

#### 4.1 Requirements Analysis and Review

Before product development, compliance analysis and business risk analysis are conducted. Compliance analysis evaluates the legitimacy of the business at the compliance level based on applicable laws and regulations, industry standards, and management measures. ZEGO invests sufficient security resources in development to ensure the capability to meet business-related compliance requirements. Risk analysis is a business security risk analysis activity based on common security risks identified through industry research and the organization's internal knowledge base, producing fundamental business security requirements to guide subsequent security design work.

After requirements analysis, internal meetings are convened with members in different roles to conduct security requirements review, discussing and confirming each security requirements analysis result. Security requirements that do not meet the requirements are optimized and adjusted following the PDCA closed-loop approach, ensuring that security requirements analysis results satisfy compliance requirements and effectively control security risks.

#### 4.2 Design Phase

Based on fundamental security design principles such as separation of duties, least privilege, and defense in depth, the ZEGO development team conducts product security architecture design using security requirements analysis results as input and common software architecture as the foundation. By referencing the STRIDE threat modeling methodology, ZEGO reduces the opportunity for attackers to exploit potential weaknesses or vulnerabilities to minimize the attack surface, avoiding risks from security features failing to cover security and privacy protection requirements.

ZEGO employs multiple identification methods, including attack intent identification, high-risk functional module identification, and attack trees, to systematically assess various threats that software may face, producing threat assessment results that reflect actual conditions.

#### 4.3 Coding Phase

ZEGO has published general and language-specific secure coding standards internally. Developers follow the corresponding coding standards based on their chosen development language, strictly implementing requirements such as security isolation elements and standardized parameter naming during the coding phase, ensuring that source code properly implements both business functions and security functions in compliance with standards while meeting business and security requirements, reducing the risk of producing insecure code during the coding and development phase.

When developers create accounts on development and test servers, approval and authorization are required, ensuring one account per person. Each developer uses the minimum and necessary permissions to complete their work, complying with the principle of least privilege. For environment isolation, physical or logical isolation is employed to strictly separate development, pre-production, and production environments. Production environment real data is not used in non-production environments, ensuring production data security and reducing the risk of data leakage.

#### 4.4 Testing and Maintenance Phase

Based on threat assessment results, ZEGO conducts security work on products including but not limited to verification of security feature implementation, verification of security policy implementation, verification of threat mitigation measures, and security implementation testing.

When security testing detects security vulnerabilities in products, ZEGO follows a vulnerability management process of discovery, classification, tracking, and remediation in a closed loop, avoiding the risk of releasing products with known vulnerabilities. If products involve third-party development components, ZEGO also regularly verifies and synchronizes security patches and updates provided by third parties, protecting products from known vulnerabilities in third-party components.

Finally, all ZEGO services or products undergo complete security testing before formal release. Only after test results receive internal review approval can they be officially launched.

#### 4.5 Source Code Security

The ZEGO team protects source code through multiple security measures including account management, access control, version iteration control, and security auditing, ensuring source code integrity, preventing unauthorized modifications and access to source code, and preventing issues that could directly affect software code security.

### 5 Operations and Maintenance Security

#### 5.1 Access Control

##### 5.1.1 Secure Area Access Control

ZEGO has established clear secure area access control policies, implementing access control management for physical areas. Based on information asset sensitivity, ZEGO divides company areas into zones, establishing isolated areas at different levels. Each area has an access control system, with entry and exit permissions controlled based on personnel roles, preventing unauthorized personnel from entering and causing system operation interruptions, equipment loss or damage, or data theft or tampering. For network area access control management, ZEGO deploys access control devices such as firewalls at network boundaries and between network zones, adopting a combination of whitelist and blacklist access control policies to control the content and protocols of data entering and leaving the network, establishing reliable network boundaries.

##### 5.1.2 Employee Access Control

ZEGO configures an endpoint security management system based on the zero trust principle and has established an internal account lifecycle management (LCM) process to update identity information and status (with account ID as the unique identifier) for all internal employees and third-party personnel accounts, including the process from employee onboarding, transfer, to departure, as well as third-party personnel from collaboration, sharing, to cooperation termination. At the same time, ZEGO determines appropriate access control rules, access rights, and restrictions for specific employee roles, clearly manages identity permissions, defines access policies, anticipates risks, enables the right people to access the right resources in different scenarios, prevents unauthorized access, and protects critical assets.

##### 5.1.3 Customer Access Control

ZEGO services support customers specifying access regions to comply with the laws and regulations of different countries or regions. Data and message transmission within the products will be restricted to ZEGO servers within the specified region. ZEGO currently supports customers independently designating access regions, requiring only that stable, highly available data center nodes exist in the region. The specific regional scope, number of nodes, network quality, and other parameters can be customized according to customer requirements. Once a customer restricts the access region, customer audio and video data and messages will not access servers outside the specified region.

#### 5.2 Vulnerability Management

ZEGO has established a mechanism for vulnerability identification, risk assessment, vulnerability handling, and reporting, to reduce the possibility of vulnerabilities in information systems and their supporting hardware and software being exploited by external threats. ZEGO actively monitors well-known public vulnerability databases, open-source communities, and security websites to stay informed of vulnerability information related to ZEGO products, enabling timely identification and reporting of information system vulnerabilities. In addition, ZEGO regularly procures third-party penetration testing services to review product security from an external perspective, identify potential vulnerabilities and weak points in products, and promptly remediate them to strengthen system security.

At the same time, ZEGO assesses the severity of identified vulnerabilities and determines remediation priority based on the risk assessment results of how the vulnerability could be exploited in ZEGO products or systems and the potential impact. For high-priority vulnerabilities, ZEGO develops and implements remediation plans. After remediation is completed, an internal vulnerability handling report is produced for knowledge retention, and vulnerability remediation information is published to customers when necessary.

Throughout the vulnerability handling process, ZEGO strictly controls the scope of vulnerability information, sharing it only among personnel involved in vulnerability remediation, to reduce the risk of attacks by others during the remediation process.

#### 5.3 Change Management

ZEGO maintains strict change control for operating systems, application software, information processing facilities, and systems, including identification and recording of significant changes, change planning and testing, change impact assessment, change request approval, and rollback plans. Some changes are standardized and deployed automatically using ZEGO's proprietary cloud efficiency system, reducing risks from manual operations and making the security risks from changes orderly and controllable.

#### 5.4 Logging and Event Management

ZEGO records and regularly reviews system user activities, anomalies, faults, and security events. For logs involving user personal information, with user authorization, ZEGO collects log information for fault analysis following the "minimum use" principle. At the same time, as logs are a key factor in event tracing, they are retained in accordance with local laws and regulations.

ZEGO has established management and response procedures for security events, with a management responsibility system to ensure security event handling procedures are fully implemented within the organization. ZEGO classifies security events based on their impact on the company or customers, and processes security events following event reporting, investigation and handling, and corrective action procedures. After fault resolution or taking necessary measures to reduce impact, the cause, type, loss, and responsibility of the incident are determined. Those responsible for information security incidents caused by violations of the company's information security policies, procedures, and security regulations are penalized in accordance with internal management rules. At the same time, ZEGO has established event reporting procedures in accordance with national or local laws, regulations, and standards to fully meet compliance requirements.

#### 5.5 Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Management

To ensure that ongoing business operations are not interrupted when sudden or gradual outage events may cause organizational operations and delivery to be disrupted, ZEGO has developed a Business Continuity Plan (BCP) for the entire organization, and regularly reviews and revises this plan when there are significant changes in the internal and external environment. 2. Business Continuity Strategy and Procedures: Based on business impact analysis results and the "cost-risk balance principle," ZEGO develops and implements business continuity recovery strategies and procedures.

#### 5.6 Configuration Management

ZEGO employs a configuration management mechanism of baseline establishment — change control — audit and verification, ensuring configuration consistency and security, and avoiding risks caused by configuration errors.

1. Establishing configuration baselines and classification management rules: ZEGO defines security configuration standards (such as operating system account permissions, firewall rules, log levels, etc.) for servers, network devices, and cloud resources, develops standardized configuration templates, and forms configuration baselines that cannot be arbitrarily modified. At the same time, configuration priorities are classified by core business systems/non-core systems and production environments/test environments, with stricter controls for core system configurations.

2. Full-process configuration change control: Any configuration change requires a submission request, clearly stating the reason for the change, impact scope, and rollback plan. Approval levels are matched based on the risk level of the change (low/medium/high). High-risk changes require joint approval from technical, business, and security teams. Execution uses canary releases or phased rollout. On failure, predefined rollback plans are triggered, and the full chain of change information (requester, approver, execution time, content) is recorded.

3. Configuration audit and verification: Automated tools scan configuration status and compare against baselines to identify "configuration deviations" (such as unauthorized open ports, abnormal permissions). Regular reviews verify whether configurations comply with compliance standards, producing audit reports and tracking remediation.

ZEGO procures third-party IaaS services from internationally renowned cloud service providers and deploys multiple server nodes globally. In compliance with the regulatory requirements of the regions where business is conducted, server nodes are allocated based on the principle of geographic proximity and current load conditions during usage. At the same time, combining public cloud capabilities with distributed server node deployment, ZEGO has developed solutions for real-time detection and defense against DDoS attacks to ensure continuous service availability.

ZEGO has developed cloud server security baselines tailored to its security needs, covering login restrictions, service permission minimization, patch updates, and other areas, and regularly updates and audits the security baselines to ensure alignment with business needs. At the same time, ZEGO configures knowledge-based and behavior-based Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) to monitor and analyze intrusion processes, and integrates risk remediation work (including weak passwords, patches, vulnerabilities, etc.) into host management processes to protect host security.

As public cloud services are a key factor supporting business, ZEGO collaborates with multiple international cloud service providers and regularly reviews or evaluates audit reports published by the third-party service providers used, ensuring cloud service security and reliability. #### 6.2 Network Security

ZEGO employs a mature and reliable three-tier network architecture to ensure important production networks are isolated from office networks. Each security domain has network firewalls implementing fine-grained network access control, and intrusion detection (IDS) security protection facilities are deployed at key network positions to detect network attack behavior, enabling early detection of anomalies and reducing the risk of security incidents. At the same time, ZEGO deploys proactive security defense measures in office networks including honeypots, access control, permission restrictions, and intrusion prevention technology (IPS) to detect and prevent internal and external threats.

ZEGO creates resource zones for each customer and establishes identity-based and role-based access control policies, allowing only user groups that need to access resources to do so, ensuring resource isolation between different customers and protecting the independence of different customer resources as well as the security of the resource processing environment.

In addition, ZEGO configures hardware Trojan and vulnerability detection for network and endpoint devices, disables USB ports on server equipment, and implements other device security control measures to prevent computer network viruses from invading through physical devices and causing data leakage or other security risks.

#### 6.3 Physical and Environmental Management

To ensure that information systems are not disrupted by natural or human factors during information processing activities, which could lead to activity interruption, data leakage, or other information security incidents, ZEGO provides independent physical isolation for areas supporting critical business processes (such as proprietary data centers), and configures infrastructure such as access control systems (controlling entry and exit permissions based on personnel roles), fire alarm systems, and camera surveillance systems to avoid security threats and environmental hazards. At the same time, equipment supporting critical business processes is configured with UPS (uninterruptible power supplies) to protect against power failures or other electrical anomalies.

### 7 Data Security and Privacy Protection

#### 7.1 Data Lifecycle Management

To ensure the normal operation of network systems and maintain data confidentiality, availability, and integrity, ZEGO implements applicable technical and management security control measures throughout the entire data lifecycle (including data collection, transmission, storage, processing, sharing, and destruction), ensuring that data tampering, loss, and leakage do not occur during data flow, which could harm customer data security. Security control measures for each stage are as follows:

1. Data Collection: Taking local laws and regulations as the baseline, ZEGO follows the principles of "minimization" and "business necessity," fully informing customers in the privacy policy of the purpose, scope, and usage methods of data collection, and only collecting business data after obtaining customer authorization and consent. At the same time, ZEGO ensures data integrity and authenticity through input validation, identity verification, and other methods; and uses TLS security protocols to ensure data transmission is not tampered with or stolen, guaranteeing the authenticity of authentication servers.

    User data collected by ZEGO's customers, such as App login information, account information, etc., is the responsibility of the customer to secure and maintain, and is not retained on ZEGO's platform.

2. Data Transmission: In different business scenarios, ZEGO employs data transmission security control measures including TLS/SSL channel encryption, AES/RSA data content encryption, signature verification, and entity identity authentication at both ends of the transmission channel, as well as review and monitoring of data transmission security policy changes, to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of data during transmission from multiple perspectives.

3. Data Storage: Following the "minimize storage time" data storage principle, ZEGO establishes different data retention period policies based on the sensitivity of collected customer personal information, and anonymizes or destroys data that exceeds the retention period. At the same time, ZEGO implements encrypted data storage and develops data backup and storage strategies in accordance with local compliance requirements. If customers submit special requests regarding modification, provision of copies, or deletion of their own data, ZEGO will cooperate with customers in data processing while meeting compliance requirements.

4. Data Use: Following the "purpose limitation" data use principle, ZEGO uses collected data only for the purposes stated in the privacy policy and does not use it for precise user profiling, automated information system decision-making, or other data processing activities without user authorization.

    At the same time, following the "minimum use" principle, ZEGO clearly defines the purpose of data use in different business scenarios, controls the scope of use through access control and encryption measures, and ensures that data use does not exceed the scope agreed upon with customers, except in cases of customer consent or to meet legal requirements and public interest. Based on core business scenarios, data importance, and other factors, ZEGO implements isolation controls for data, system functions, and operating environments, ensuring that different customer data remains independent and that the data processing environment is secure.

    All of ZEGO's data use activities are logged. Automated tools, log auditing, and other methods are used to effectively identify and monitor unauthorized data use, and regular assessments are conducted on the impact of data use purposes, scope, and methods on personal information security, ensuring the legitimacy, necessity, compliance, and security of data use.

5. Data Sharing: ZEGO follows the principles of "minimization" and "necessity" for both internal and external data sharing.

    When sharing data internally, while ensuring that internal sharing is legitimate, lawful, and necessary, ZEGO ensures the security of transmission methods (such as channel encryption, software/hardware encryption), controls access permissions based on business roles, and strengthens data protection awareness to mitigate the risk of data being stolen or compromised by various types of threats during transmission. At the same time, ZEGO has deployed a zero trust endpoint management platform internally to monitor and audit employee data sharing behavior, ensuring no unauthorized operations or unauthorized external disclosures occur.

    When sharing data externally, ZEGO informs users in the privacy policy about external recipients, the content and purpose of data sharing, and only provides related content externally after obtaining authorization and consent. Before data sharing, ZEGO also signs a Data Protection Agreement (DPA) with the third party, clearly defining the purpose, scope, and method of data use, as well as the third party's data protection responsibilities, to protect user data security.

6. Data Destruction: In scenarios such as service termination, expiration of the retention period, withdrawal of consent by the data subject, achievement of the processing purpose, inability to achieve the purpose, or the purpose no longer being necessary (unless local laws and regulations have special requirements), ZEGO processes stored data through hard destruction or soft destruction. If the data to be destroyed involves third parties, ZEGO will also notify relevant third parties in writing to destroy the data and track the data processing results.

#### 7.2 Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)

As a cloud communication service provider, when developing new products or services, making changes, or when significant changes occur in the external environment, ZEGO conducts privacy risk identification based on product features, national or local data protection laws and their related guidelines, and conducts privacy impact assessments on personal information processing activities. Based on assessment results, current business conditions, the threat environment, laws and regulations, and standard requirements, ZEGO continuously adjusts the boundaries of personal information protection and modifies security control measures, keeping personal information processing within a state of controllable risk.

#### 7.3 Data Classification and Risk Assessment

Combining data type characteristics and business operation needs, ZEGO defines data classification principles and methods, has established a data classification management system, and defines different control requirements for different levels of information assets in areas such as information access control, information publishing, labeling, and storage.

At the same time, based on risk elements including assets, risks, security measures, threats, and vulnerabilities, and referencing international risk assessment standards and management specifications, ZEGO analyzes information asset value, potential threats faced by assets, and existing protective measures, determines the probability of security incidents and potential losses, identifies ZEGO's security risks, develops corresponding control measures based on risk assessment results, and regularly follows up and reassesses to achieve dynamic monitoring and closed-loop management of risks.
