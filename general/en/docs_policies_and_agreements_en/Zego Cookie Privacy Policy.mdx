# ZEGO Cookie Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: March 13, 2021**

Your Privacy

When you visit our website, we use cookies to ensure you have the best possible experience on our website. These cookies may relate to you, your preferences, or your device. This information does not usually directly identify you but can provide you with a more personalized website experience. We respect your right to privacy, and you may choose to disable certain types of cookies. However, blocking certain types of cookies may affect your website experience and the services we are able to provide.

We need to use cookies to remember the choices you make in our cookie privacy manager. This will have the following consequences:

 - If you delete all your cookies, you will need to update your preferences with us again.

 - If you use a different device or browser, you will need to inform us of your preferences again.

ZEGO Technology will act as the data controller and process your personal data as described below.

Purpose of Processing and Legal Basis

When you visit our website, we collect and process cookie information from your browsing of our website. We process this information to identify whether you are a returning visitor to our website, analyze the behavior of visitors to our website, enhance the communication functionality and architecture of our website, and create a profile of your personal interests, which may also allow us to display relevant advertisements for our products and services to you on other websites. If possible, we will also combine your online behavioral data with the personal data you last provided to us.

This Cookie Policy describes how we use cookies and similar technologies to identify you when you visit our website. It describes what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to authorize us to use them. In certain circumstances, we may use cookies to collect personal information, or if we combine it with other information, it may constitute personal information.

#### 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files that are stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make websites work better or more efficiently and to report information. Cookies set by the website owner are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies allow third-party features (such as advertising, interactive content, and analytics) to be provided on or through the website. The parties that set these third-party cookies can recognize you when you visit the relevant website or other websites.

#### 3. How Can You Control Your Cookies?

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject the collection of cookies by the website. You can set permissions for cookie collection through the options in your cookie settings. The options in the cookie settings allow you to choose to accept or reject categories of cookies being collected. You cannot reject strictly necessary cookies, as they are strictly necessary to provide services to you. The cookie settings options can be found in the notification banner and on our website. If you choose to reject cookies, you may still use our website, but some features and areas of the website may be limited. You can also set or modify your web browser settings to accept or reject cookies. Since the method for rejecting cookies through web browser settings varies by browser, you should visit your browser's "Help" menu for more information. You have the right to choose whether to accept cookies. To opt in or out of cookies, click on our pop-up banner when you first visit the website. Please note that if you choose to reject the installation of cookies, you may not be able to use the full functionality of the website.

You can update your preferences at any time — most devices (such as when using mobile applications) and browsers (such as when using web applications and pages) allow you to change your cookie settings. These settings are typically found within the "Options" or "Preferences" menu of your browser. The following links provide information about managing cookies on commonly used browsers. You can also use the "Help" option in your browser for more detailed information.
- [Google Chrome](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/61416?co=GENIE.Platform%3DDesktop&hl=en)
- [Microsoft Edge](https://privacy.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-10-microsoft-edge-and-privacy)
- [Mozilla Firefox](https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/enable-and-disable-cookies-website-preferences)
- [Microsoft Internet Explorer](https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/17442/windows-internet-explorer-delete-manage-cookies)
- [Opera](https://www.opera.com/help/tutorials/security/privacy/)
- [Apple Safari](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/safari)
